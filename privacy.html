Privacy Policy — ssh.ai

Last updated: 2026-05-11 · App: ssh.ai (Android, ai.eight24family.sshai), version 1.0.x · Distributor / maintainer: Eight 24 Family LLC · nikita@eight24family.ai

ssh.ai is an Android client that lets you SSH into your own servers and drive the Claude Code, Codex, or Gemini CLI tools that live there. Everything in this policy stems from one design rule: we don't want copies of your data and we never run intermediary servers between your phone and your hosts.

What we do NOT collect

  • No accounts. We don't ask you to register.
  • No chat contents. Prompts and replies travel directly between your phone and your SSH server.
  • No SSH credentials. Passwords, private keys, and FIDO security-key credential handles stay on your device, encrypted at rest with androidx.security.crypto (AES-256, hardware-backed via Android Keystore where available).
  • No advertising IDs. No GAID, no device fingerprint.
  • No location, contacts, calendar, microphone, or camera.

What we DO collect (opt-out available)

For stability we send anonymous technical data to Sentry. Toggle off any time via Settings → Privacy → Crash reporting.

Identifiers are stripped before sending: Sentry's installation UUID is dropped in beforeSend (see SshAiApp.initSentryIfEnabled); IPs are scrubbed at Sentry's project-level PII config. Sentry stores this data for 90 days.

Country / city geo derived from IP. Sentry resolves an approximate geographic region (country and city level — not GPS) from the source IP at ingestion time and stores that, then drops the IP itself. This is the only piece of geolocation Sentry holds. It cannot be turned off independently of crash reporting — opt out of crash reporting if you don't want it.

What stays on your device only

  • All SSH server records, hostnames, usernames.
  • All SSH keys (passwords, PEM private keys, FIDO key handles).
  • All chat messages.
  • Locally-cached copies of your AI session JSONL files.

Data on your servers

Anything stored on the servers you connect to is governed by your server, your AI account, and the agent's policy (Anthropic for Claude, OpenAI for Codex, Google for Gemini). ssh.ai doesn't read or proxy that traffic — it's just a terminal in your pocket.

Third-party services

  • Sentry — crash and performance reporting. The in-app toggle stops all SDK calls.
  • Anthropic / OpenAI / Google — only via the CLI tool you choose to run on your server, with your credentials. We are not a party to that traffic.

We do not use Google Analytics, Firebase Analytics, Crashlytics, AppsFlyer, Adjust, Branch, or any advertising/attribution SDK.

Hardware security keys (FIDO / CTAP2)

If you import a hardware security key for SSH authentication, the app uses Android NFC and USB-host APIs to talk to the key over CTAP2. The credential handle is stored encrypted on the device. We do not collect biometric data. A "touch" is the physical-presence check the FIDO standard requires — a cryptographic signature, not an image or fingerprint template.

Permissions the app requests

  • Internet — to reach the SSH servers you add and (if crash reporting is on) Sentry.
  • Foreground service + persistent notification — to keep agent sessions alive while the screen is locked or the app is backgrounded.
  • Notifications — to show approval prompts and connection status.
  • NFC / USB host — only when you tap to use a hardware security key.

We do not request location, contacts, calendar, microphone, camera, SMS, or call-log permissions, and the app does not declare any of them.

Children

ssh.ai is designed for software developers and is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children.

Your rights / data deletion

  • Right to opt out (crash reporting + geo derivation): Settings → Privacy → Crash reporting off. Takes effect on the next app launch.
  • Right to erasure (GDPR Art. 17): Settings → Privacy → Delete all my data. Removes the encrypted server registry, all stored keys (including hardware-key credential handles), all session caches, the on-device upload cache, all preferences, and the Android Keystore master key, then terminates the app process. To remove anonymous telemetry already received by Sentry (within their 90-day retention window), email nikita@eight24family.ai.
  • Right of access (GDPR Art. 15): email below. With the PII strippings in place, we typically have nothing user-identifying to return.

International transfers

Sentry processes data in the EU and the US. By keeping the crash-reporting toggle on, you consent to this transfer.

Changes to this policy

If we change what the app collects, we'll update this page and bump the Last updated date. Material changes will also be surfaced in-app on first launch after update.
